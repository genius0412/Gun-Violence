3. Economic Consequences on Sciety
- All the money drains out and very little money comes in
- Low home ownerships
- Home value decrease due to shootings
"Paid $250,000 for their house and now they can't sell it for $100,000, because of the violence."
3. Economic Consequences on Society
Fewer retail and service establishments
- People leave the area to spend money on shopping or services
- Fewer job opportunities
Happens more often in black and brown communities
3. Economic Consequences on Society
- Creates an environment in which people feel unsafe in public places
- Impacts human rights to religion, education, and leisure
Creates a negative stereotype against black and brown people and the place they live in, causing the cycle to keep going
Solutions?
Total ban of guns!
3 Step Solution for Total Ban of Guns
1. Changing the majority opinion through one on one interactions
2. Organizing actions in order to make small changes to laws
3. Creating a national movement
1. Changing the majority opinion
One on one interactions
- Only 19% of American citizens want a total ban on handguns
- Connecting the people in that 81% with victims one on one
- Social media ads
2. Organizing actions in order to make small changes to laws
Effects on society requires laws to enfore the change
- Voting for Congress people who support enforcing gun laws
- Sending emails to their representatives
- Signing petitions about gun laws
- Starting protests to make changes to the law
3. Creating a national movement
- Need laws to enforce a total ban of guns
- Gradual changes in each community will add up to a total ban of guns nationally
- Community leaders can collaborate with each other to make a movement
- Pressure the Supreme Court, the president, and other major leaders
